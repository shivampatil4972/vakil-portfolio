Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 1, 2026

Effective Date: June 1, 2026

Welcome to the website of Adv. Ashwanith Chelekar ("we", "our", "us"). We are committed to protecting the privacy of individuals who visit our website and consult with us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website at www.advashwanith.com.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information:

  • Personal Identification Information: Name, email address, phone number, and any other details you provide through our contact or booking forms.
  • Case-Related Information: Details about your legal matter that you voluntarily share during an inquiry or consultation request.
  • Usage Data: Browser type, IP address, pages visited, time spent on pages, and referring URLs collected automatically through cookies and analytics tools.
  • Communications: Emails, WhatsApp messages, or any correspondence you initiate with us.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your consultation requests and inquiries.
  • To schedule and confirm appointments.
  • To send important legal updates, newsletters, or firm announcements (with your consent).
  • To improve the functionality and user experience of our website.
  • To comply with applicable legal and professional obligations.

3. Attorney-Client Privilege

Any information you share with us in the context of seeking legal advice may be protected by attorney-client privilege under applicable Indian law. We treat all client communications with strict confidentiality in accordance with the Bar Council of India Rules.

4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, trade, rent, or share your personal information with third parties except:

  • When required by law, court order, or government authority.
  • To trusted service providers (e.g., email services, web hosting) who assist in operating our website, under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • With your explicit prior consent.

5. Cookies

Our website may use cookies to enhance your browsing experience. These include:

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Required for the website to function (e.g., theme preference, language toggle).
  • Analytics Cookies: Used to understand how visitors interact with our website (e.g., Google Analytics). You may opt out via your browser settings.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. Inquiry data is typically retained for a maximum of 3 years.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate information.
  • Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations).
  • Withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time.

9. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to external websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those sites. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

10. Changes to This Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy at any time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of our website after any modifications constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
